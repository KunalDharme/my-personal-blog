The Hounds of Baskerville

March 12, 2011

Sherlock and I were approached by Henry Knight, a man haunted by the memory of his father being attacked by a gigantic hound on Dartmoor. While Sherlock was skeptical, he took the case—mostly out of boredom.

We traveled to Baskerville, a military base near the moor, and uncovered rumors of genetic experiments and psychological warfare. Sherlock was visibly rattled for the first time I’d ever seen—something truly shook him.

As we dug deeper, it became clear that what Henry saw was not a real beast, but the result of a powerful hallucinogenic gas and a conspiracy involving the base itself. Sherlock set up a trap and tricked his own mind into believing the hound was real to understand the victim's terror.

In the end, the truth was more disturbing than any supernatural story: fear weaponized.
