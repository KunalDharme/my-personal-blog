A Study in Pink

January 14, 2010

This was my first real case with Sherlock Holmes. The police were baffled by a series of suicides, all committed in similar fashion. But Sherlock instantly suspected something more sinister.

We visited the latest scene – a woman's body in a derelict house, dressed immaculately. Sherlock deduced that she had scratched a name onto the floor before she died. "Rache" — a clue or a message?

The case twisted and turned rapidly, with Sherlock always two steps ahead. He eventually faced the killer – a cabbie who played a deadly game, forcing victims to choose between two pills, one harmless, one fatal.

He offered Sherlock the same choice. I stepped in at the last moment... with a gun I hadn’t used since Afghanistan.

That day, I became more than a flatmate. I became a part of something incredible.
